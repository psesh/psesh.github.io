About me.

I live in London and work at the University of Cambridge and The Alan Turing Institute. I studied computational engineering at Cambridge and aerospace engineering previously at Maryland. During my doctorate I worked at Rolls-Royce plc in Derby and spent time at Stanford. My research focuses on uncertainty quantification, optimisation and machine learning applied to address challenges in aeronautics. I am the founder of Effective Quadratures.

My current research projects are:
  • Bayesian models for improved aerothermal flow-physics in engines
  • Subspace-based dimension reduction: algorithms and 3D blade design
  • Machine learning with polynomials: from quadratures to neural networks
  • Leveraging virtual reality for digital twins in aeronautics
