Pranay Seshadri


I live in London and work at the University of Cambridge and The Alan Turing Institute. I studied engineering at Cambridge and previously at Maryland. My research focuses on uncertainty quantification, optimization and machine learning applied to address challenges in aeroengines. I am the founder of Effective Quadratures.

My current research projects are:
  • Bayesian models in aeroengines
  • Subspace-based dimension reduction: algorithms and design
  • Machine learning with polynomials: from quadratures to neural networks
